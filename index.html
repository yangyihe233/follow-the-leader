Abstract

Fixed-length continuum robots are limited in reachable workspace and in their ability to perform follow-the-leader (FTL) motion in confined environments. This paper presents a rod-driven, bellow backbone extensible continuum robot that achieves bending, extension, and retraction through six absolute rod length inputs, without requiring a separate insertion actuator. To model this coupled deformation and deployment behavior, we formulate an extensible piecewise curvature (ePPC) framework that maps absolute rod lengths to curvature, bending plane angle, and deployed segment length while incorporating length-dependent bending limits. Based on this model, a closed-loop incremental QP controller is developed for bidirectional tip tracking and FTL motion. Both segment-wise and coordinated FTL strategies are implemented under the same variable-length framework. Experiments demonstrate accurate bidirectional tip tracking and whole body shape following along planar and spatial paths, validating the proposed design, model, and control framework.

Contribution

To address these limitations, this paper proposes a rod-driven extensible continuum robot(RDECR) with a bellows backbone. In the proposed design, the rods serve as the active driving elements, while the bellows backbone provides structural support and geometric constraint. Without requiring additional insertion modules or segment-level extension actuators, the robot achieves bending, extension, and retraction through coordinated absolute control of rod lengths.
